“KEY STEPS TO INSPECT YOUR TYRES AFTER A POTHOLE IMPACT”

Key Steps to Inspect Your Tyres After a Pothole Impact.

Hitting a pothole can be an unpleasant experience, and beyond the initial jolt, your tyres may suffer serious damage. A damaged tyre can compromise safety, performance, and fuel efficiency. To ensure your vehicle stays roadworthy, follow these key steps to inspect your tyres after a pothole impact.

1. Check for Visible Damage

The first step after hitting a pothole is to visually inspect your tyres. Look for:

  • Sidewall bulges – A bulge indicates internal damage, which can lead to a blowout.
  • Cuts or cracks – Even small cuts can expand over time, weakening tyre integrity.
  • Tread separation – If parts of the tread appear loose or detached, replacement is necessary.

2. Assess Tyre Pressure

A sudden drop in tyre pressure can be a sign of slow air leaks or punctures. Use a tyre pressure gauge to compare the readings with the manufacturer’s recommended PSI. Driving on underinflated tyres can lead to poor handling and increased wear.

3. Check for Rim Damage

Your wheels also absorb the impact, so inspect the rims for:

  • Bends or dents – Even a minor dent can cause vibrations and misalignment.
  • Cracks – A cracked rim is unsafe and should be replaced immediately.

4. Look for Alignment Issues

A pothole can knock your wheels out of alignment, affecting the way your car handles. Signs of misalignment include:

  • Steering pulling to one side
  • Uneven tyre wear
  • Vibrations in the steering wheel

If you notice any of these, it’s best to get a wheel alignment check from a professional.

5. Test for Suspension Damage

Pothole impacts can also affect your suspension system. Listen for strange noises, excessive bouncing, or a rough ride. If your vehicle feels unstable, a shock absorber or suspension component might be damaged.

6. Take a Test Drive

After a thorough inspection, take your car for a short drive. Pay attention to:

  • Unusual vibrations
  • Steering responsiveness
  • Any warning lights on the dashboard

If anything feels off, consult a professional tyre and wheel service to diagnose the problem properly.

Final Thoughts: Stay Safe on the Road

Potholes are unavoidable, but regular tyre inspections and maintenance can help prevent long-term damage. By following these key steps, you can identify potential tyre and wheel issues early and keep your vehicle safe. If you suspect serious damage, don’t delay—get your tyres checked by a professional to avoid costly repairs down the road.

Stay alert, inspect your tyres, and drive safely!
